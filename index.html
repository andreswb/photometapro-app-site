Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add photos to process?

Click "Add Photos" (‚åòO) or drag and drop photos directly into the main window. PhotoMetaPro supports JPEG, PNG, TIFF, RAW (CR2, CR3, NEF, ARW, DNG, ORF, RAF, RW2), and HEIC formats.

How do I create and save templates?

Fill in the metadata fields in the left sidebar (copyright, keywords, client info, etc.), configure your output settings, then click "Save as Template" at the bottom. Your template will be saved with all settings for future use.

What is Quick Edit and how do I use it?

Quick Edit lets you modify metadata for individual photos without batch processing. Right-click any photo thumbnail and select "Quick Edit Metadata" to instantly edit its copyright, keywords, location, GPS coordinates, and description.

How does GPS reverse geocoding work?

When you enter GPS coordinates in your template, click "Get Location Name" to automatically convert them to a readable location (e.g., "New York, NY, USA"). You can also use "Detect GPS" to extract coordinates from photos that already contain location data.

What are selection filters and how do I use them?

Use the "Select by Status" menu to filter photos by their processing state: Select Pending (not yet processed), Select Completed (successfully processed), or Select Failed (encountered errors). Use "Hide Completed" to remove finished photos from view and focus on remaining work.

How do I view processing statistics?

After processing photos, click the chart icon in the toolbar to view detailed statistics including total processed, success rate, processing time, and average time per photo. You can export a text summary for your records.

What metadata does PhotoMetaPro edit?

PhotoMetaPro edits EXIF data (date/time), IPTC data (copyright, keywords, credit, location, description), TIFF data (copyright, artist, custom fields), and GPS data (latitude/longitude coordinates with automatic location names).

Will my original photos be modified?

No. PhotoMetaPro creates new processed files in your chosen output directory. Your original files are never modified. You can also enable "Backup Original Files" to create copies in an 'originals' subfolder for extra safety.

What are the three filename patterns?

Choose how processed files are named: (1) filename_processed.jpg - adds '_processed' suffix, (2) filename_templatename.jpg - uses your template name, (3) filename_20260105.jpg - adds the current date. Change this in Output Settings.

What happens if a file already exists in the output directory?

Choose from three strategies: Auto-rename (adds _1, _2, etc. - recommended), Overwrite existing files (use with caution), or Skip existing files. Set your preference in Output Settings.

Can I add custom metadata fields?

Yes! In the template editor, scroll to "Custom Fields" section. Add any field name and value you need. These are written to the TIFF metadata dictionary with a 'Custom_' prefix and also stored in IPTC Supplemental Category.

What does "Preserve Original Metadata" do?

When enabled, PhotoMetaPro keeps existing camera metadata (EXIF, GPS) and merges your new metadata with it. When disabled, only your new metadata is written. Generally, keep this enabled to preserve camera settings and original GPS data.

What does "Preserve File Dates" do?

When enabled, processed files keep the same creation and modification dates as the original files. This is useful for maintaining chronological organization in your photo library.

How do I access recent templates quickly?

Your last 3 used templates appear at the top of the templates list labeled "Recent" for one-click loading. This saves time when switching between common projects or clients.

How do I use recent output directories?

Your 3 most recently used output directories appear under "Recent:" in Output Settings. Click any folder to instantly set it as your output directory without using the file picker.

What do the colored borders on photos mean?

Photo thumbnails have color-coded borders showing their status: Gray (pending/not processed), Blue (currently processing), Green (successfully completed), Red (failed with error). Hover over failed photos to see the error message.

How do I view what metadata will be applied?

Click the eye icon in the toolbar to preview all metadata that will be written to your photos before processing. This shows TIFF, EXIF, IPTC, and GPS data in an organized format.

Where can I see processing logs?

Click the list icon in the toolbar to view detailed processing logs. Filter by status (success, warning, error, info) and export logs as JSON. The log records every operation with timestamps.

Can I cancel processing if I made a mistake?

Yes, click "Cancel" or press Escape during processing. Photos already processed remain in the output directory, but no new photos will be processed. You'll see how many were completed before cancellation.

How do I duplicate a template?

Click the document icon next to any saved template to create a copy. This is useful for creating variations of existing templates without starting from scratch.

How do I import/export templates?

Click the three-dot menu (‚ãØ) next to "Project Templates" and select "Import Templates" or "Export Templates". This saves templates as JSON files for sharing between computers or backing up your configurations.

What JPEG quality should I use?

The default 90% provides excellent quality with reasonable file sizes. Use 95-100% for archival or maximum quality (larger files), or 85% for web use with minimal quality loss. Adjust the slider in Output Settings.

Privacy Policy

No Data Collection

PhotoMetaPro does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data, photos, or usage information. All processing happens entirely on your Mac.

Local Processing Only

Your photos never leave your device. PhotoMetaPro processes all images locally on your Mac. No images, metadata, or any other data is uploaded to servers, transmitted over the internet, or shared with third parties.

What is Stored Locally

PhotoMetaPro stores the following data locally on your Mac using macOS UserDefaults:

  • Saved templates: Your metadata configurations (copyright text, keywords, client info, GPS coordinates, custom fields, output settings)
  • Recent templates list: IDs of your 3 most recently used templates
  • Output directory bookmarks: Secure references to your chosen output folders
  • Recent output directories: Paths to your 3 most recently used output locations
  • Processing settings: JPEG quality, file naming strategy, filename pattern preferences
  • Processing logs: Filenames, timestamps, and processing status for operations you've performed
  • Application preferences: Window size/position, welcome screen settings, language preference

This data is stored exclusively on your Mac and is never transmitted anywhere. It's used only to provide features like template management, recent items access, and processing history.

File Access Permissions

PhotoMetaPro requires macOS permissions to:

  • Read photos: To access and process the images you explicitly select through the file picker or drag-and-drop
  • Write to directories: To save processed photos to your chosen output location
  • Security-scoped bookmarks: To remember your output directory selection between app launches

You maintain complete control over which files and folders PhotoMetaPro can access through macOS's App Sandbox security features. PhotoMetaPro can only access files and folders you explicitly grant permission to.

No Network Access

PhotoMetaPro does not connect to the internet or any external services. The app operates completely offline. The only exception is the optional GPS reverse geocoding feature, which uses Apple's system-level CoreLocation framework to convert coordinates to location names - this is handled by macOS itself, not by PhotoMetaPro.

No Analytics, Tracking, or Telemetry

We do not collect:

  • Usage analytics or statistics
  • Crash reports or diagnostic data
  • Feature usage tracking
  • Error reporting
  • Performance metrics
  • Any identifiable information

PhotoMetaPro has no ability to monitor your usage, track your behavior, or report any information about you or your photos.

Your Complete Control

You have full control over all data:

  • Delete templates: Click the trash icon next to any template to permanently remove it
  • Clear logs: One-click clearing of all processing logs from the log viewer
  • Reset preferences: Uninstall PhotoMetaPro to completely remove all locally stored data
  • Export data: Export templates and logs as JSON files for backup or transfer

When you uninstall PhotoMetaPro by deleting it from your Applications folder, all associated data stored in UserDefaults is automatically removed by macOS.

Security Best Practices

PhotoMetaPro follows Apple's security guidelines:

  • App Sandbox: Enabled for secure, isolated operation
  • Security-scoped bookmarks: Safe, encrypted references to user-selected directories
  • Minimal permissions: Only requests necessary file access permissions
  • Code signing: Digitally signed by Apple to verify authenticity
  • Notarization: Verified by Apple for malware and security issues
  • No third-party code: No analytics SDKs, tracking libraries, or external dependencies

Children's Privacy

PhotoMetaPro does not knowingly collect any information from children. The app is designed for professional and personal photo management and does not have features directed at children.

Third-Party Services

PhotoMetaPro does not integrate with, share data with, or communicate with any third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or external APIs (except for Apple's system-level CoreLocation for optional GPS reverse geocoding).

Data Retention

All data is retained only on your local device for as long as you keep the app installed. You can delete any data at any time through the app interface. Uninstalling the app removes all associated data.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy to reflect app updates or clarify our practices. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. The core principle will never change: PhotoMetaPro does not collect, transmit, or share your data.

Your Rights

Since PhotoMetaPro does not collect or transmit any personal data, there is no data to access, correct, or delete on our end. All your data remains on your device under your control.
